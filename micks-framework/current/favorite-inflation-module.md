# Micks Picks — Favorite Inflation and Failure-to-Cover Module

Status: Permanent
Effective date: 2026-07-12
Applies to: All future market refreshes, card grading, writeups, API records, website releases, parlays, and post-card reviews.

## Purpose

Elite and heavily bet teams can continue winning outright while repeatedly failing to cover inflated spreads or run lines. Reputation, public support, recent wins, and championship expectations can cause the market to charge more than the team's true margin profile supports.

The Micks Picks system must distinguish:

- probability of winning outright;
- probability of covering the posted spread or run line;
- price paid for the moneyline;
- expected winning margin versus market-implied margin; and
- whether the market has already fully priced the team's quality.

A strong team is not automatically a strong bet. A favorite that wins but does not cover is a market-pricing problem, not evidence that the handicap was close enough.

## Required Progressive Pattern Scan

Before any favorite is graded B or higher, scan its most recent 5, 10, and 20 applicable games, separated by market type.

Track at minimum:

1. Straight-up record.
2. ATS or run-line record.
3. Closing spread or run-line price.
4. Final margin.
5. Margin versus closing expectation.
6. Consecutive cover failures.
7. Cover rate as a favorite.
8. Cover rate in the same spread band.
9. Home/road split.
10. Rest, travel, and back-to-back context.
11. Public ticket percentage when available.
12. Handle or respected-money percentage when available.
13. Opening line versus closing line.
14. Whether recent wins were one-score, one-run, overtime, late-rally, or non-covering wins.
15. Whether the team is being priced from brand reputation rather than current margin performance.

Do not combine moneyline wins and spread covers into one trend. They answer different questions.

## Favorite Inflation Alert Levels

### Level 1 — Caution

Trigger when any two are true:

- failed to cover 3 of the last 5;
- covered fewer than 45% of the last 10;
- two consecutive non-covers as a favorite;
- average final margin trails the average closing spread by at least 1.5 points or 0.5 runs;
- public tickets exceed 70% and the line moves against the favorite or does not strengthen;
- current spread is at least 1.5 points or 0.5 runs worse than the recent comparable market band.

Required response:

- reduce confidence;
- prohibit an automatic A grade;
- document the inflation concern in Market and Number Context;
- compare moneyline, first half, first quarter, first five, team total, derivative, live-entry, and pass alternatives.

### Level 2 — Strong Inflation

Trigger when any one is true:

- failed to cover 4 of the last 5;
- failed to cover 6 of the last 8;
- covered 40% or less over the last 10 as a favorite;
- three or more consecutive non-covers while still winning most games outright;
- average margin underperformance is at least 3.0 points in basketball or 0.75 runs in baseball;
- the closing number repeatedly worsens despite the team failing to cover;
- the favorite has lost at least 1.5 units across its last five ATS/run-line bets at standard staking.

Required response:

- maximum pre-override grade: B;
- maximum standard stake: 0.50u;
- spread or run line cannot be VIP;
- do not use the inflated spread/run line as a parlay leg;
- route to an alternate market, live trigger, Watchlist, or Pass unless independent current-game evidence clearly overcomes the pattern.

### Level 3 — Progressive Fade / Market Tax

Trigger when any two are true:

- failed to cover 5 of the last 6;
- covered 30% or less across the last 10 comparable favorite spots;
- four consecutive cover failures;
- negative ATS/run-line ROI of at least 20% over the last 10;
- repeated outright wins by margins materially below the closing requirement;
- public support remains above 75% while the spread continues to inflate;
- current price is the worst number in the team's recent comparable range without a material lineup upgrade.

Required response:

- spread/run line is an automatic Pass unless the user explicitly overrides;
- favorite may only be considered through a lower-variance derivative if its price still offers value;
- opposing spread may be considered only after independent matchup validation; never fade solely because a streak exists;
- exclude the favorite from Lotto Parlays and Pick of the Day;
- require an explicit Favorite Inflation Alert in the writeup and tracker.

## Automatic Grade and Unit Adjustments

Apply after the normal matchup grade is calculated.

- Level 1: reduce one half-grade where practical, or reduce confidence by 0.5 to 1.0 points.
- Level 2: reduce at least one full grade; cap at B and 0.50u.
- Level 3: spread/run line becomes Pass; derivative maximum is B- and 0.25u unless the user explicitly overrides.

An A or A- favorite spread/run-line play requires all of the following:

- no Level 2 or Level 3 inflation alert;
- at least 50% cover rate in a meaningful recent sample or a clear reason the old sample is no longer representative;
- current lineup, starter, role, or matchup improvement that directly supports margin creation;
- the current number is not worse than the historical comparable band;
- at least two independent paths to a cover;
- no unresolved public-tax or reverse-line-movement concern.

## Alternate-Market Routing

When the favorite is still likely to win but the cover profile is poor, evaluate in this order:

1. Moneyline, only if the price is not prohibitively expensive.
2. First half or first quarter, if the team's strongest edge is early.
3. First to 10 or first to 15, if early shot quality and starting-unit execution are the edge.
4. First five innings, if the starting-pitcher edge is stronger than the bullpen or full-game margin edge.
5. Team total, only when the scoring path is independently supported.
6. Opponent spread, only when matchup evidence supports competitive game probability.
7. Live entry at a specified number or game-state trigger.
8. Pass.

Do not convert an overpriced spread into an overpriced moneyline parlay merely to preserve action.

## Parlay Safeguards

- A Level 2 or Level 3 inflated favorite may not be used as a spread/run-line parlay leg.
- A moneyline version may be used only when the combined price remains acceptable and the favorite has a strong outright-win profile.
- Do not stack the same inflated team across a straight bet, derivative prop, and parlay.
- Parlays must disclose when a leg was converted from spread/run line to moneyline because of cover-pattern risk.
- Two teams with active Favorite Inflation Alerts cannot be paired together in the same official parlay.

## Writeup Requirement

Every flagged favorite writeup must include:

- recent straight-up performance;
- recent ATS or run-line performance;
- whether the team is winning without covering;
- current number versus recent comparable numbers;
- the exact alert level;
- the grade or unit deduction applied;
- why the chosen derivative is better, or why the game is a Pass;
- the condition that would remove or reduce the alert.

Approved label:

**Favorite Inflation Alert:** The team remains capable of winning outright, but its recent margin profile has not justified the market's spread or run-line tax.

Do not use vague language such as “they have been costing us.” Quantify the cover failure and price problem whenever verified data is available.

## Reset and Recovery Rules

The pattern is not permanent. Reduce or remove the alert when:

- the team covers 3 of 4 comparable favorite spots;
- its 10-game cover rate returns above 50%;
- average margin performance again meets or exceeds the closing requirement;
- a material roster, injury, rotation, pitching, coaching, or tactical change invalidates the prior sample;
- the market materially corrects the spread or run-line price; or
- the current matchup presents a demonstrably different margin profile.

A single cover does not automatically reset a Level 2 or Level 3 alert.

## Team Watchlist Initialization

The New York Liberty and Los Angeles Dodgers must enter the next market refresh as monitored teams, not automatic fades.

For each, the system must verify current 5-, 10-, and 20-game ATS/run-line performance before assigning any new spread or run-line grade. Until that review is complete:

- no A-grade Liberty spread;
- no A-grade Dodgers run line;
- no Liberty spread or Dodgers run line in an official parlay;
- moneyline or derivative alternatives require price validation;
- reputation and championship status cannot substitute for cover evidence.

This watchlist status is evidence-seeking, not a permanent blacklist.

## Publishing Validation

Before publishing any favorite graded B or higher, confirm:

1. Progressive Pattern Scan completed.
2. Alert level recorded or explicitly marked None.
3. ATS/run-line data is separated from straight-up data.
4. Grade and unit adjustment applied.
5. Alternate markets evaluated.
6. Parlay restrictions enforced.
7. Writeup explains the market tax and failure path.
8. Current line remains inside the no-bet cutoff.
9. No unsupported trend statistics are displayed.
10. The final endpoint preserves the alert fields.

## Governing Rule

The progressive pattern system must protect the bankroll from repeatedly paying a premium for elite teams that are winning games but failing to beat the number. Team quality establishes the baseline; market price and cover probability determine whether the wager belongs on the card.
