# Micks Picks — NBA Summer League Verification Module

Effective date: 2026-07-14

Status: Permanent controlling module for all NBA Summer League and NBASL research, grading, card construction, props, and parlay exposure.

## Triggering post-card review

On July 13, 2026, Minnesota Timberwolves -5.5 lost 111-84 to Portland. The 27-point outright loss also caused the Cavaliers ML + Timberwolves ML + Dream ML parlay to lose.

This was not classified as ordinary late-game variance, a backdoor cover, or a narrow number miss. It was classified as a personnel and rotation-verification failure. The original read relied too heavily on projected rotation strength without enough confirmed evidence about the active roster, recent minutes, primary creation, and expected rest decisions.

## Core rule

Projected roster or projected rotation strength is never sufficient by itself for an official NBA Summer League wager.

A Summer League side may become official only after the following verification gate is completed:

1. Confirm the active roster or latest available availability report.
2. Review who played meaningful minutes in the most recent game.
3. Identify the expected primary ball-handler and at least one secondary creator.
4. Check for expected rest, shutdowns, late scratches, or role reductions.
5. Verify that the presumed rotation advantage is supported by actual usage or recent minutes, not name recognition alone.
6. Obtain at least one independent matchup confirmation from a current source, market signal, verified beat report, or trustworthy game-board model.
7. Confirm that the current number remains inside the listed cutoff after availability information is known.

If any critical item is missing, the play must be downgraded to B-, Watchlist, Live Only, or Pass.

## Grade and unit limits

- Maximum standard grade: B.
- Maximum standard stake: 0.50u.
- B requires the full verification gate.
- B- is the maximum when one non-critical confirmation remains incomplete.
- No Summer League side may qualify for VIP, A-, A, or A+.
- Normally release no more than two official Summer League sides on one card.
- After a severe personnel-read miss, temporarily limit the next slate to one verified Summer League side until the verification process is demonstrated successfully.

## Spread rules

- Spreads of -5.5 or higher require confirmed rotation continuity and a verified margin path.
- Do not describe a team as deeper or stronger without naming the actual expected creators, role players, or recent-minute evidence supporting that claim.
- Prefer the moneyline, a smaller derivative, Live Only, or Pass when the team edge appears real but the margin is difficult to verify.
- A large favorite is not playable merely because the opponent roster looks weaker on paper.

## Parlay rules

- An unverified Summer League side may not be used as a parlay leg.
- A Summer League side already released as a straight wager should not also be included in the main parlay unless the duplicate exposure is explicitly approved and counted in the team exposure cap.
- Do not use a heavily priced Summer League favorite merely to increase a parlay payout slightly.
- Every Summer League parlay leg must independently pass the same roster, minutes, creation, rest, and price checks as a straight wager.

## Writeup requirements

Every Summer League writeup must identify:

- confirmed or expected active personnel;
- recent meaningful-minute players;
- primary and secondary creation;
- the specific rotation or matchup edge;
- the exact number and cutoff;
- the cashing path;
- the shutdown, rest, substitution, or backdoor failure path;
- why the play remains B or lower.

Generic phrases such as “better projected roster,” “deeper rotation,” or “stronger on paper” are not sufficient without supporting personnel detail.

## Post-card classification rules

Use these classifications when reviewing Summer League failures:

- Number variance: correct side read, narrow spread miss or late backdoor.
- Rotation variance: unexpected but plausible substitution pattern after adequate pregame verification.
- Availability failure: a late scratch, shutdown, or rest decision materially changed the handicap.
- Personnel-read failure: the expected roster or creation advantage was not supported on court.
- Market-read failure: the price overstated the verified edge.
- Duplicate-exposure failure: the same weak read caused losses in both a straight wager and parlay.

Severe outright losses by favored teams should not be dismissed as Summer League randomness. They require a documented review of what information was missing and whether the wager should have been Watchlist or Pass.

## Operational enforcement

Before publishing a Summer League card, the final review must state:

- number of official Summer League sides;
- total Summer League units;
- verification status for each side;
- whether any side appears in a parlay;
- any missing roster or minutes confirmation;
- the reason each play is not a Pass.

This module supersedes any earlier rule allowing an official Summer League side based primarily on projected rotation quality.
